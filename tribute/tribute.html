Rani of Jhansi

A symbol of resistance to the British Raj for Indian Nationalists.

Here is a timeline of Rani Laxmibai's life.

  • Nov, 1828 - Born in the town of Varanasi. Named Manikarnika Tambe. Her education included shooting, horsemanship, and fencing
  • May, 1842 - Married to the Maharaja of Jhansi. Called Rani Lakshmibai.
  • Nov, 1853 - The Maharaja passed away and his only heir was an adopted son. The East India Company applied the Doctrine of Lapse
  • Mar, 1854 - Rani Laxmibai was given an annual pension and ordered to leave the palace and fort
  • May, 1857 - The Rani asked the British political officer for permission to raise a body of armed men for her own protection during the Indian Rebellion
  • Jun, 1857 - Rebels of the Bengali Native Infantry seized the star fort of Jhansi and massacared 40-60 European Officers, along with their families
  • Jul, 1857 - The Rani's forces defeated mutineers who attempted to assert the claim of Sadashiv Rao on the throne
  • Aug, 1857 - The start of 6 months of peaceful rule under Rani Laxmibai
  • Mar, 1858 - British forces arrived to a well defended Jhansi. The Rani defended Jhansi against the siege
  • Apr, 1858 - The Rani was forced to leave the city with Damodar Rao. She joined the forces of Tatya Tope
  • May, 1858 - The Rani was defeated by British forced in Kalpi. The trope fled to Gwalior
  • May, 1858 - Nana Sahib was proclaimed the Peshwa. The Rani wanted to prepare to defend the city but found no support
  • Jun, 1858 - The Rani tried to leave Gwalior but her troops were attacked and she succumbed to injuries.
'She lived and died for her country, We cannot forget her contribution for India.'
-Colonel Malleson
